About Me

I am Riyadh, a Doctoral Researcher at the CISPA Helmholtz Center for Information Security in Saarbrücken, Germany. I hold a degree in Computer Science from Saarland University and began my research journey at CISPA in 2024. Currently, I am a member of Trusted Systems Group leads by Sven Bugiel.

My research is situated at the intersection of Mobile Security, Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), and Usable Security. I am particularly focused on enhancing privacy-preserving mechanisms within the Android system. In the broader context of Trusted Computing and Usable Security, my work aims to explore and improve the holistic user experience in privacy-related decision-making processes.

Through my interdisciplinary approach, I strive to bridge the gap between technical security measures and user-centric design, ensuring that privacy and usability go hand in hand.
